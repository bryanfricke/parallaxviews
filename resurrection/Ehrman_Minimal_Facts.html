4 + 1 Minimal Facts — Bart Ehrman’s Affirmations

Clickable sources, collapsible details, and copyable quotes.

About Bart Ehrman

Bart D. Ehrman is a New Testament scholar and agnostic who affirms certain historical facts (e.g., Jesus’s crucifixion, the early disciples’ belief in his resurrection, and James the Just’s leadership) but rejects the resurrection as a historical miracle. He has frequently debated evangelical scholars and others, often arguing against the logical inference that these facts point to a literal resurrection.

Facts

  1. Crucifixion of Jesus
  2. Disciples’ appearance-claims & sincerity (Peter, James son of Zebedee, John)
  3. Paul’s appearance-claim, sincerity, and death
  4. James the Just — appearance in creed, leadership, death
  5. (+1) Empty tomb — burial in creed & early testimony re: Joseph of Arimathea
Note on later distancing: In later writings/debates, Ehrman expresses greater caution about the empty tomb tradition, while the earlier statements below reflect his stronger acceptance of early burial testimony.

Quotes & Sources

Fact 1 — Crucifixion of Jesus
“For one thing, I am convinced beyond a shadow of a doubt that Jesus was physically crucified and died on the cross. That is rock-bottom certain in my books.”
— Bart D. Ehrman, The Historicity of Jesus – A Blast from the Past. Source: ehrmanblog.org
Fact 2 — Disciples’ Claims of Appearances & Sincerity (Peter, James son of Zebedee, John)
“That Jesus’ followers (and later Paul) had resurrection experiences is, in my judgment, a fact. What the reality was that gave rise to the experiences I do not know.”
— Bart D. Ehrman, How Jesus Became God (2014), p. 182. Book info: HarperOne
“We can say with complete certainty that some of his disciples at some later time insisted that… he soon appeared to them, convincing them that he had been raised from the dead.”
— Quoted in context summarizing Ehrman’s view; see discussion: chab123.wordpress.com
“Paul reports that Jesus appeared to Peter (Cephas) … and this is in a creed he received from others, going back to just a few years after Jesus’ death.”
— Bart D. Ehrman, How Jesus Became God (2014), p. 182. Book info: HarperOne
Fact 3 — Paul’s Claim, Sincerity, and Death
“We know some of these believers by name; one of them, the apostle Paul, claims quite plainly to have seen Jesus alive after his death.”
— Bart D. Ehrman, How Jesus Became God (2014), p. 182. Book info: HarperOne
“Peter… and Paul almost certainly yes [were martyred].”
— Bart D. Ehrman, “Were the Disciples Martyred for Believing in the Resurrection?” Source: ehrmanblog.org
Fact 4 — James the Just: Appearance in Creed, Leadership, Death
“Most scholars who study such things think that James himself claimed to have seen Jesus alive after his death and that he was not basing it on the claims of others.”
— Bart D. Ehrman, “James the Brother of the Lord.” Source: ehrmanblog.org
“Yes, James was the head of the church in Jerusalem early on, not Peter. But he would not be the ‘pope’ because that is the designation of the head of the church specifically in Rome.”
— Bart D. Ehrman, The Brother of Jesus and the Book of James, on ehrmanblog.org
“James the brother of Jesus was probably martyred, though he may have been killed on other charges/for other reasons.”
— Bart D. Ehrman, “Were the Disciples Martyred for Believing in the Resurrection?” Source: ehrmanblog.org
(+1) Empty Tomb — Burial in Creed & Early Testimony (Joseph of Arimathea)
“The earliest creed about Jesus’ death and resurrection (1 Cor 15:3–4) states he was buried.”
Scripture reference: 1 Corinthians 15:3–4 (ESV)
“The earliest accounts we have are unanimous in saying that Jesus was in fact buried by this fellow, Joseph of Arimathea, and so it’s relatively reliable that that’s what happened. We also have solid traditions to indicate that women found this tomb empty three days later.”
— Bart D. Ehrman, From Jesus to Constantine: A History of Early Christianity, Lecture 4 (2003). Context: ehrmanblog.org
